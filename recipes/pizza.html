Pizza

Description

Contemporary Neapolitan pizza is an evolution of the traditional pizza from Naples. It keeps the core principles—high hydration dough, long fermentation, and high-temperature baking— but introduces a more pronounced, airy crust (cornicione) and a lighter overall texture.

The dough is typically fermented for 24 to 48 hours, resulting in better digestibility and a more complex flavor. The center remains soft and slightly moist, while the edges rise dramatically with a honeycomb structure. Toppings are often minimal and high quality, focusing on balance rather than quantity.

Ingredients

  • High-protein flour (00 or similar)
  • Water (high hydration, around 65–75%)
  • Fresh yeast or sourdough starter
  • Salt
  • San Marzano tomato sauce
  • Fresh mozzarella (fior di latte or buffalo)
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh basil

How to cook

  1. Prepare the dough by mixing flour, water, yeast, and salt. Let it rest and ferment for 24–48 hours.
  2. Divide the dough into balls and let them proof at room temperature until soft and extensible.
  3. Stretch the dough gently by hand, preserving the air in the edges to form the cornicione.
  4. Add tomato sauce, mozzarella, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  5. Bake in a very hot oven (ideally 450–500°C in a wood-fired oven) for about 60–90 seconds.
  6. Finish with fresh basil and serve immediately.
