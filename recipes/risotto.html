Risotto alla Milanese

Description

Contemporary risotto builds on traditional Italian techniques while emphasizing texture, aroma, and ingredient quality. The dish balances a creamy, slightly al dente grain with the nuanced flavor of saffron, butter, and cheese.

The key is to toast the rice lightly, gradually add hot stock, and stir continuously. This method allows the rice to release its starches, creating a smooth, cohesive creaminess without becoming mushy. Modern variations often incorporate seasonal ingredients, microgreens, or aged cheeses for an elevated presentation.

Ingredients

  • Carnaroli or Arborio rice
  • Butter
  • Olive oil
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • White wine
  • Hot chicken or vegetable stock
  • Saffron threads
  • Grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Salt and pepper

How to cook

  1. Heat stock and keep it warm on a separate burner.
  2. Sauté onion in butter and olive oil until translucent, then add the rice and toast lightly.
  3. Deglaze with white wine and let it evaporate completely.
  4. Add warm stock gradually, stirring frequently, letting the rice absorb liquid before adding more.
  5. Infuse saffron threads into a bit of stock and add midway through cooking for color and flavor.
  6. Once rice is creamy and al dente, remove from heat and stir in butter and Parmigiano-Reggiano.
  7. Season to taste and serve immediately, optionally garnished with microgreens or additional cheese.
